Q1 of 2020 was definitely a time that history will remember (and probably all 2020 will). Coronavirus is causing lots of troubles everywhere in the world, both on the social and economics side. Nonetheless, I hope that we, as humanity, will be able to learn something from all this mess and try to avoid it in the future.

On my side, since my company is working at a reduced pace, it means that during Q1 I had much more free time for learning new things and to advance with my personal projects. In this post, I would like to review what I have done in this first 3 months of the year and rebalance my goal for the next quarter.

## Q1 Goals Review
### Tea and Business Side Project
Let’s start from the ~~bad~~ not so great things: I have completely abandoned tea tasting. Probably due to this particular period, which is quite stressful for everyone, I wasn’t in the mood of coming home from work and sit to tasting tea. Nonetheless, I expect to at least start some tasting in the next quarter.

Since we are on the negative side, I have not even started buying things for the side business, but I was expecting this. In my current home, I don’t have enough space so this project is something that will probably start in the second half of the year.

### Website
Now onto the positive! I have dedicated lots of time to the website, not in term of writing content but in term of style redesign. I am almost done with it for now, and I will update final tweaks here and there when I find new ideas. The Academic theme for Hugo is definitely the best one I have found out there, due to the community behind it and the extensive support. Even with basics HTML and CSS knowledge, it is possible to style the website so that it reflects the necessity of most of the technical users.

The most complicated redesign was in the _About_ page, which I still have to complete, but all in all I am proud of the result. From now on I plan to dedicate much more time to writing content for the blog, and I already have some post scheduled. I will mainly talk about Python, embedded systems and physics so, if you are interested, stick around.

### Python and C
In February and March, I completed the “Google IT Automation with Python” professional certificate. This gave me better knowledge in Python and some other technologies (e.g. _Puppet_ for managing system configuration) that can be useful in many tech fields, even not directly related to IT. I will bring some of this knowledge on this blog in the future. (By the way, if you are interested in a review of this course, drop me a line)

On the contrary, I have not progressed with AI. I will move this goal down the line since in the last few weeks I began studying embedded development quite seriously. During my work, I have already programmed some MCU with the tools provided by some vendors but I don’t like it. So I began some research on how to create my own cross-compilation toolchain for ARM MCUs. I am doing some progress and this brought me to also become more interested in other aspects of embedded development. For the next few months, my sole and only focus will be studying embedded systems. Expect to see some posts about my progress in the next few months.

## New Goals for Next Quarter
Let’s list all the new goals for the next quarter.

- Re-introduce tea tasting in the daily routine.
- Publish regularly on the blog.
- Complete a python project to help images optimisation and integration on the blog.
- Complete the "Development of Secure Embedded Systems" specialisation on Coursera.
- Create my custom cross-compilation toolchain for embedded development on the ARM platform.

## Conclusion
All-in-all, given the strange period in which we all are living, and given the fact that the first blog post was listing goals for an entire year, I would say that I am quite on track.

If you have similar goals or want to discuss something related to these topics, drop me a line in the comments. In the meantime, stay home, please!
